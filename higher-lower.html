Entry Profitability

A Pick6 entry pays its base payout times every pick's multiplier, only when all picks hit. EV per $1 = base payout × ∏ multiplier × ∏ probability. DraftKings sets base payouts per contest — verify them in the app and adjust here (saved in your browser). Best entries chase the model's largest disagreements with DraftKings' pricing — often longshot alternate lines whose tail probabilities carry the most model risk — so treat their ROI as an optimistic ceiling, not an expectation.

Higher % is the probability the player finishes strictly above the line in the FanGraphs simulation; Lower % is strictly below. Lines marked ● are the default line DraftKings features on the card; the rest are alternate lines. The Pick chip shows the purchasable side with the best Prob × Mult (probability times payout multiplier), and Prob is that side's probability. A ✓ flags lines whose Prob × Mult clears the threshold — roughly the per-pick break-even shown in the entry panel — on any line, default or alternate. Lineup slots come from the FanGraphs lineup source for the slate (* = unconfirmed), so lineups can shift these numbers during the day. Entry math treats picks as independent and ignores voids (a pick that lands exactly on a whole-number line is voided by DraftKings, which reduces the entry to a lower pick level rather than losing).
